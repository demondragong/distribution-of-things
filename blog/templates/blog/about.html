What is a distribution anyway?
The distribution of a dataset is the spread of the data which shows all possible values or intervals of the data and how often they occur. We’re quite used to describe datasets with average values such as mean, sometimes median. These are interesting but often fail to describe a dataset accurately. Distributions don’t tell everything either, but they are a lot more powerful than a single figure.
